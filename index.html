Menu Strategy & Naming

First impressions drive ordering behavior

The Risk

Starting the menu with Bakehouse items may increase breakfast orders, but it's dangerous for lunch and dinner ‚Äî approximately ~70% of platform orders happen during those dayparts.

Options

  • Option A: Change menu order dynamically by daypart (if the platform supports it)
  • Option B (Recommended): Keep pizza first in the menu at all times to protect the majority of order volume

Rename the Brand on Platforms

Change from "Badabing Bakehouse and Pizza" to "Badabing Pizza & Bakehouse". When you say "Bakehouse and Pizza," it gives the impression that pizza is a side offering. Lead with your strongest category.

Offers Optimization

Alignment across platforms & better combo pairing

Inconsistent Offers Across Platforms

Offers are not identical on Talabat and Deliveroo. This creates confusion for repeat customers and makes performance tracking harder. Align the offers across all platforms.

Pizza Combo Needs Rework

Current: Pizza Combo = Any Pizza + Any Drink + Any Side or Bakehouse Item

Combining pizza with bakery items is not the best pairing ‚Äî it feels off-brand and doesn't drive perceived value.

Recommended Combo

Replace the bakehouse option in the combo with Potato Wedges or Chicken Tenders ‚Äî classic pizza sides that feel like a natural, high-value pairing.

Channel Economics

Per-order economics across all delivery platforms

Talabat Gems: Eating the Bottom Line

Talabat Gem orders erode the bottom line regardless of incrementality. With a 5.64% EBITDA vs. 36.53% for non-Gem Talabat orders, Gems are destroying profitability. The Gems algorithm is not beneficial ‚Äî it attracts discount-seeking customers, not loyal ones.

Recommendation

  • Cancel Talabat Gems ‚Äî the unit economics don't justify participation
  • Double down on Careem ‚Äî highest EBITDA% at 39.58% and strong CMPO%
  • Double down on Deliveroo ‚Äî solid EBITDA% at 24.38% with the highest AOV (108.03)
