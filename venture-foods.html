The Problem

Ad spend jumped +15.7% but revenue barely moved (+1.2%). Orders only grew +1.8%. We were spending significantly more to acquire marginal returns. The paid ads were not generating incremental sales — they were likely cannibalizing organic orders.

The Decision

Based on these numbers, we made the call to stop all Talabat ads in February. The goal: determine whether organic demand is strong enough on its own, and whether the bottom line improves without ad spend.

The Key Takeaway

Despite losing 190 orders and 1,568 KWD in revenue, the operational profit increased by 372 KWD. By eliminating 1,217 KWD in ad spend, the bottom line improved even with lower sales volume. The ads were costing more than they were generating.

Ardhiya is Different

Unlike the overall brand where profit increased, Ardhiya's operational profit dropped by 45.66 KWD (-6.0%). The 187 KWD saved in ad spend was not enough to offset the revenue loss. This suggests ads at Ardhiya may have been driving genuinely incremental orders — not just cannibalizing organic ones.

What This Means

Ardhiya is the branch most at risk from the no-ads strategy. If the overall bottom line starts to dip, Ardhiya is likely the first place we'd consider reinstating targeted ads. This branch needs the closest monitoring.

Monitoring & Emergency Protocol

We're keeping our eyes on — ready to act if needed

Emergency Threshold

If the operational profit drops below a critical threshold, we will immediately schedule an emergency call and reinstate Talabat ads. The bottom line is the ultimate decision-maker — not vanity metrics like orders or revenue.

What We're Tracking Daily

  • Operational Profit (weekly) — the single most important metric right now
  • Order volume trend — watching for stabilization or continued decline
  • Branch-level revenue — identifying which locations need attention
  • Avg. basket size — ensuring we're not losing higher-value customers

Current Status

Watching closely — Bottom line is currently above threshold. Profit improved +6.4% in the first week without ads. We continue to monitor and will act decisively if the trend reverses.
